Dataset and Thesis
22,156 Targetable Locations. 48 Active Platforms. A Market Where Fragmentation and Institutional Validation Coexist.
The U.S. veterinary services market is principally acquisition-led. The independent base is broad and geographically distributed, while de novo opportunities are more concentrated and require market-level demand validation before deployment. The highest-quality acquisition candidates are not in empty markets; they sit in established local service areas with manageable competitive overlap, measured platform presence, and sufficient independent density to support a structured outreach program.

Investment Thesis
Acquisition-Led Market
The independent base is broad and distributed nationally, creating a target-rich environment for platform entry and add-on sourcing at scale.
Proven Markets, Not Empty Ones
The strongest candidates sit in established local service areas with manageable overlap and enough independent density to support outreach. Pure whitespace is a smaller subset.
Platform Activity Varies by Region
Active platform counts by region identify where consolidation has taken hold versus where the market is still largely independent. Light activity often marks better entry conditions than zero activity.
De Novo Is Selective, Not Broad
De novo pockets exist in targeted markets, primarily Midwest and Southeast. Population coverage, doctor availability, and local economics all require validation before treating any market as a greenfield candidate.

Geographic and Competitive Intelligence
Middle-Band Markets Hold the Deepest Acquisition Pool. Light Platform Activity Marks the Best Entry Conditions.
Proximity band and platform activity are the two most useful structural filters for sequencing a sourcing program. Z2 and Z3 markets account for the majority of Strong/Priority candidates and produce the highest average opportunity scores. On platform activity, light presence consistently outperforms both zero and heavy: it validates demand without compressing the independent supply available for outreach.
Figure 2   Targets and Avg. Opportunity Score by Proximity Band
Z1 = immediate overlap  ·  Z2 = local catchment  ·  Z3 = secondary reach  ·  Z4 = regional isolation
Z2 holds 9,713 targets, the deepest pool in the dataset, with the highest average opportunity score of 69.9. Z4 markets score 50.9 on average and are better screened for de novo than acquisition.
Figure 3   Targets and Avg. Opportunity Score by Platform Activity
None = zero nearby platforms  ·  Light = 1-2  ·  Moderate = 3-4  ·  Heavy = 5 or more
Light activity scores highest (70.7)
Platforms nearby validate local demand without crowding the independent supply available for acquisition outreach.
Heavy activity signals competitive pressure (58.1)
Relevant for tuck-in strategy but not the broadest whitespace lane. Prioritize sourcing in None and Light markets first.
Proximity bands (Z1-Z4) reflect distance to nearest competing clinic. Exact radius definitions are market-type dependent and available on request. Corporate Development
Six Sourcing Lanes Translate Market Data Into Actionable Strategy.
The dataset supports six distinct sourcing lanes, each suited to a different acquisition or expansion objective. Selecting the right lane determines which targets receive outreach first, which enrichment fields matter most, and whether the campaign is optimized for volume, conviction, or selective expansion.
01   Core Acquisition
Strong/Priority targets in Z2 and Z3 bands
Broadest initial outreach cohort. These targets combine proven market demand, manageable competitive overlap, and the highest average opportunity scores. First destination for Clay enrichment and outreach sequencing.
PE Use Case
Platform entry, buy-and-build, and broad add-on programs.
02   Fragmented Cluster
Immediate-overlap areas with multiple independents and manageable platform activity
Several independent clinics in close proximity without a dominant platform present. Each clinic alone may be smaller, but the cluster supports a roll-up thesis. Requires local owner mapping before outreach.
PE Use Case
Roll-up and tuck-in programs; density-based platform construction.
03   Validated Platform-Active
Light or moderate platform activity with strong independent density
Platform presence validates demand and market quality without crowding the independent supply. Highest average opportunity scores in the dataset. Best entry conditions for platforms moving into a new geography.
PE Use Case
Platform entry and regional expansion where thesis validation already exists.
04   Selective De Novo
Good de novo markets with sufficient population coverage and limited nearby supply
Greenfield expansion screen for platforms with de novo capability. Concentrated in Ohio, Indiana, North Carolina, Texas, and select Midwest CBSAs. Requires demand and doctor availability validation before any site commitment.
PE Use Case
Greenfield expansion layer after priority acquisition markets are confirmed.
05   Reputation-Backed
Strong/Priority markets with high review count and strong rating
Local winners with visible client loyalty and proven community presence. These 5,026 targets are the highest-confidence first-call pool in the dataset. Particularly useful for Northeast and Southeast targeted outreach.
PE Use Case
High-conviction outreach; suited for platforms with near-term LOI targets.
06   Digital Uplift
Strong/Priority markets with low review visibility or weak digital footprint after enrichment
These 2,657 targets may be under-marketed rather than underperforming. They represent a proprietary-sourcing pool with post-acquisition improvement potential across digital, marketing, and client retention.
PE Use Case
Value creation thesis; platforms with operational playbooks for digital improvement.
Final Takeaways
The vet market remains target-rich and fragmented. The best opportunities are in proven local service areas, not fully empty markets.
Light platform activity is more attractive than zero. It validates demand while still leaving room for independent sourcing.
Southeast is the deepest sourcing region. West has strong acquisition signal but requires population validation. Midwest and Northeast offer strong local market selectivity.
De novo is real but selective. The broader opportunity is acquisition-led, with de novo serving as an expansion layer after priority markets are confirmed.
