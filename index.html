The strongest CBSAs have enough top-tier targets to justify near-term outreach and enough above-average targets to support follow-on sourcing. The six decision signals below define what separates a useful sourcing market from a high-scoring outlier in an otherwise shallow market.

What De Novo Score Uses (vs. STOC Geo Index)
De novo logic: supply gap, density, population
Positive when a trade area has unmet demand relative to existing supply and population. Driven by whitespace, not target quality.
Acquisition logic: competitive position, pressure, proximity
Positive when a clinic has strong local standing in a market with manageable competition. Driven by target quality, not whitespace.
Why they can point in opposite directions
A dense market with many clinics to acquire is often a poor de novo market because supply already meets demand. Chicago and Atlanta are both diligence-heavy acquisition markets and poor de novo candidates.

Analytical safeguard
These relationships are directional screens, not proof of causality. They should guide where to investigate, not as a causal model of market performance. Use them to build the sourcing argument, not to overclaim precision.
Interpretation Safeguards
Raw priority-label undercount → Use STOC Geo Index tiers
Prevents the opportunity from looking artificially tiny. Use 65+ and 59+ thresholds instead of the raw dataset label.
Region-size overclaim → Compare by depth, pressure, CBSA quality
Avoids saying larger regions are better just because they are larger. Depth, average score, and pressure profile all matter.
Clinic-density fallacy → Treat density as helpful only when pressure is manageable
Avoids overvaluing crowded markets. High density + high pressure = diligence-heavy, not automatically attractive.
White-space fallacy → Keep de novo whitespace separate from acquisition
Prevents low-supply markets from being mistaken for buy-and-build markets. Empty markets require de novo logic, not acquisition sourcing.
Platform-context overclaim → Anonymized context only, not targets
Preserves clean target definitions. Platform names excluded throughout; platform rows are never counted as acquisition inventory.
Digital-signal misuse → Keep reviews/ratings out of geo-opportunity narrative
Avoids irrelevant correlations (ρ < ±0.03). Digital maturity belongs in a separate enrichment section, not the market screen.
